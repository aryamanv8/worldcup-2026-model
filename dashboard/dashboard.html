Where the two disagree — and why we don't trade it

The gap is one-directional: the market concentrates probability on elite names, the model spreads it toward the mid-tier. On liquid markets that's favorite-longshot bias meeting a calibrated model — interesting to see, but not a tradeable edge. This is the model's view, not a signal.

Method

Poisson goals model with a Dixon–Coles correction, fit on 2000–2026 internationals with Elo, squad-value and form features. Temperature-calibrated (T = 0.77), then run through a 50,000-path Monte Carlo of the 48-team bracket.

Market prices are de-vigged per round to the slot count; model and market are always shown as two distinct estimates.

Honest footnote

This is a calibrated forecast and a study of market efficiency, built for learning. The model's numbers are not betting advice, and the "model vs market" gaps are a view, not signals. No edge survived the costs.
